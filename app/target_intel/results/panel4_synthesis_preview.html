Panel 4 · MTAP

Synthesis & thesis

Taking Figures 1–4 and the Section 3 trial table together, what does the current MTAP / PRMT5 landscape suggest, and what are the most defensible next steps?

Thesis

The MTAP / PRMT5 axis appears to have moved from a 2016 synthetic-lethality concept into a clinical platform now entering its first pivotal trials. The question is no longer whether MTA-cooperative PRMT5 inhibition is active in MTAP-null disease — Rodon 2024, Engstrom 2023 and Belmontes 2025 are broadly supportive of that view — but in which patient populations, with which combination partners, and with what biomarker calling, durable responses are likely. The early ~92% ORR reported for vopimetostat + daraxonrasib in MTAP-deleted RAS-mutant PDAC (Tango, June 2026; n = 12 evaluable, single-arm, non-peer-reviewed) is among the most encouraging signals released so far; if it holds up in larger and peer-reviewed reporting, it would reposition PDAC as a leading near-term opportunity for the axis. Pivotal Phase 2/3 readouts (MountainTAP-30 in PDAC; planned vopimetostat + daraxonrasib Ph3) are the next inflection point.

Patient-selection strategy

  • Homozygous MTAP deletion is currently the most defensible enrolment biomarker; heterozygous loss likely does not qualify.
    Figure 1 indicates that MTAP homdel is most prevalent in GBM (~43%), MESO (~36%), BLCA (~23%), PAAD (~21%), ESCA (~18%), LUSC (~16%), SKCM (~14%) and HNSC (~11%). Figure 3 suggests these focal homdel events (median footprint ≈ 1.5–2.8 Mb, < 3% arm-level) are mechanistically distinct from heterozygous loss, which is predominantly arm-level passenger (median 25–115 Mb, 56–86% arm-level). The mechanistic consequence — MTA accumulation sufficient to partially inhibit PRMT5 — is generally expected to require complete MTAP loss, so heterozygous events would not normally be expected to qualify a patient.
    Figure 1 Figure 3 Kryukov GV 2016
  • Companion diagnostics may need calibration against panel-vs-WES under-call.
    A cross-check against the MSK-IMPACT 50K real-world panel-sequenced cohort (Figure 1 footnote; rank-Spearman ≈ 0.81) shows the indication ranking replicates, but absolute homdel rates appear 5–18× lower in panel-based GISTIC −2 than in whole-exome ABSOLUTE. Trial enrolment based on commercial NGS panels alone may therefore under-enrol the true MTAP-null population. IHC for MTAP loss has been reported as a viable orthogonal biomarker in NSCLC and could help bridge that gap.
    Aldea M 2026
  • CDKN2A is a frequent co-deletion surrogate but probably not the right primary biomarker.
    Figure 4 shows CDKN2A appears to be co-deleted in ~88–100% of MTAP-homdel patients across cohorts with at least 5 deleted patients, with the focal 9p21 neighbourhood (IFN cluster, ELAVL2) frequently pulled in. CDKN2A alone may serve as a surrogate where MTAP testing is unavailable, but the metabolic phenotype that drives the PRMT5 / MAT2A dependency is MTAP-specific.
    Figure 4 Zhang H 1996
  • Oncogene-driven NSCLC subsets appear under-appreciated for MTAP enrichment.
    Aldea 2026 reports MTAP loss in ~27–33% of ALK-rearranged, ~18–26% of RET-rearranged, and ~17–24% of EGFR-mutant NSCLC — substantially higher than the pan-cohort NSCLC figures in Figure 1 (~10% LUAD, ~16% LUSC). In EGFR/ALK preclinical models the authors report that driver-TKI plus MTA-cooperative PRMT5 inhibition (BMS-986504) may outperform either agent alone, which — if it holds in patients — could define a distinct clinical-development lane.
    Aldea M 2026

Combination-therapy logic — by indication

Pancreatic adenocarcinoma (PDAC) prevalence: ~21%
  • Vopimetostat (TNG462) + daraxonrasib (RAS(ON) multi-selective) → ~92% ORR in 12 MTAP-del RAS-mut patients (June 2026, single-arm, non-peer-reviewed).
  • BMS-986504 + nab-paclitaxel + gemcitabine (MountainTAP-30, Ph2/3) — ASCO GI 2026 TPS.
The per-cohort mutational data behind Section 3 indicate that the MTAP-homdel PAAD population is relatively uniform (KRAS ~85%, TP53 ~70%, SMAD4 ~30%), and the MTAP-homdel profile largely mirrors the cohort baseline. Combinations that target KRAS or that ride a chemotherapy backbone therefore appear to double-target the dominant biology rather than introduce new mechanistic risk.

Non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) prevalence: ~10% LUAD · ~16% LUSC (TCGA primary); ~17–33% in ALK/RET/EGFR-driven subsets
  • BMS-986504 + pembrolizumab + chemo, 1L MTAP-del NSCLC (NCT07063745, Ph2/3).
  • MTA-cooperative PRMT5i + driver-TKI for oncogene-addicted NSCLC (preclinical rationale, Aldea 2026).
  • AMG 193 dose-expansion in MTAP-del NSCLC (WCLC 2024 P3.12D.11).
The per-cohort mutational data behind Section 3 show MTAP-homdel NSCLC patients have a heterogeneous driver profile (TP53, EGFR in LUAD; KMT2D, PIK3CA, NFE2L2 in LUSC). The oncogene-driven subsets identified by Aldea 2026 are MTAP-enriched and pair naturally with established TKIs, which makes driver-TKI combinations a reasonable next-step hypothesis even though clinical validation in those subsets is still early.

Urothelial carcinoma prevalence: ~23% (TCGA-BLCA primary tumour)
  • IDE397 + sacituzumab govitecan (Trodelvy®): ~33% → ~57% ORR (DL1 → DL2) in MTAP-del urothelial (IDEAYA Sep 2025, press release).

Key risks & open questions

  • Acquired resistance has been reported in preclinical MTAP-null models — clinical translation is unknown.
    An April 2026 bioRxiv preprint describes mechanisms of acquired resistance to MTA-cooperative PRMT5 inhibition in MTAP-null NSCLC, with resistant clones showing collateral MEK-inhibitor sensitivity. These observations are preclinical and not yet peer-reviewed; clinical resistance data remain to be published. Resistance-mapping work likely needs to run in parallel with Phase 3 readouts.
    [bioRxiv preprint authors — pending peer review] 2026
  • Panel-based CN-calling may under-enrol MTAP-null patients across the field.
    The MSK-IMPACT cross-check in Figure 1 indicates panel GISTIC −2 calls run ~5–18× lower than whole-exome ABSOLUTE, and panel coverage of MTAP probably varies by sequencing centre. Companion diagnostics for MTA-cooperative PRMT5i trials would likely benefit from being validated against orthogonal methods (IHC, MTA mass-spec, MAT2A pathway activity) rather than panel CN alone.
  • Several expected co-occurring drivers are mutually exclusive of MTAP-homdel, not co-occurring.
    An exploratory per-cohort Fisher analysis (held in the audit trail) suggests that TP53, RB1, and CDKN2A point-mutations are systematically mutually exclusive with MTAP-homdel — consistent with these being alternative routes to the same p53 / RB / cell-cycle escape rather than independently co-selected events. IDH1 is also mutex in LGG, reflecting that the IDH-mutant glioma branch is a genomically distinct disease from the IDH-wild-type / 9p21-deleted branch. Trial designs that assume MTAP-del × TP53-mut co-occurrence could therefore mis-estimate eligible populations; the actionable co-occurrences with MTAP loss appear to be on the CN side (CDKN2A / CDKN2B / 9p21 neighbours), not the mutation side.
    Figure 4
  • Activity differentiation between MTA-cooperative PRMT5i and MAT2A inhibition is not yet resolved.
    Kalev 2021 supports a mechanistic distinction (MAT2A inhibition reduces SAM and would be expected to deepen PRMT5 suppression in MTAP-null cells), but no head-to-head clinical trial currently exists. IDE397 + Trodelvy (urothelial) and BMS-986504 monotherapy have each reported response signals in their respective settings, but the data are not directly comparable (different indications, combination versus monotherapy, small and non-peer-reviewed n). Whether MAT2A inhibition delivers a meaningfully different therapeutic window or is best used in combination with — rather than instead of — MTA-cooperative PRMT5 inhibition is likely to remain unresolved until a head-to-head or sequential trial is run.
    Kalev P 2021

Worth exploring next

  • Consider a two-tier companion-diagnostic strategy: panel CN as eligibility screen + MTAP IHC confirmatory.
    Likely reduces the panel-vs-ABSOLUTE false-negative rate (Figure 1 cross-check) and provides an orthogonal call that should be resilient to MTAP probe-coverage gaps.
  • Prioritise PDAC and oncogene-driven NSCLC for new programme investment.
    PDAC currently has the largest reported absolute response signal (TNG462 + daraxonrasib ≈ 92% ORR in 12 patients, June 2026 — still early and non-peer-reviewed) and oncogene-driven NSCLC is the most under-appreciated MTAP-enriched subset (Aldea 2026), pairing naturally with established TKIs.
  • Build resistance / biomarker-evolution mapping in parallel with Phase 3.
    Pre-clinical resistance signals (April 2026 bioRxiv preprint) and a lineage-agnostic splicing signature following PRMT5 inhibition (March 2026 bioRxiv preprint) suggest that PD biomarkers and resistance mapping would best be designed into Phase 3 from the outset rather than retro-fitted after readout.
